<objective>
Deliver HARD-SEC-01: the OpenWeather `appid` (API key) must never escape into an
exception message, traceback, or log line. Belt-and-suspenders per D-01/D-02:

1. **Source fix (D-01)** — at both `client.py` raise sites (`fetch_onecall`,
   `geocode`), catch the key-bearing `httpx.HTTPStatusError`, redact `appid=<value>`
   → `appid=***`, and re-raise a fresh `httpx.HTTPStatusError(..., from None)` that
   preserves the type contract (`.response.status_code` intact).
2. **Backstop (D-02)** — wrap `_LiveStderr.write` (`__init__.py`) with the same
   redaction helper so any future/forgotten leak is scrubbed at the single stderr
   choke point shared by both `structlog.configure` sites.
3. **Regression suite** — assert the sentinel key is absent from `str(exc)`, the
   full traceback, and captured stderr for all three leak paths (onecall, geocode,
   Discord `on_message`), plus a `.response.status_code` type-contract canary.

Purpose: close the reproduced F12 end-to-end leak (a failing `!weather <loc>` over
Discord dumps the key-bearing traceback) and make the leak unreachable at the source
rather than patching each logging site.

Output: `weatherbot/_redact.py` (new), redacted re-raise in `client.py`, the
`_LiveStderr.write` backstop in `__init__.py`, and `tests/test_redact_hygiene.py`
(new). No behavior change beyond redaction; no new dependencies.
</objective>
